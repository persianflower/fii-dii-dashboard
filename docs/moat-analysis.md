EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
  The FII/DII dashboard has no meaningful moat as a standalone tool. The new features (AI summary, sector breakdown, F&O data, monthly rollup,
  Telegram alerts) increase utility but none are durably defensible. The only real moat is the integration with the user's existing toolchain
  (NSE Sentiment Analyzer, Portfolio Risk Scanner) — combining FII/DII context with personal portfolio risk and stock sentiment.

BUSINESS UNDERSTANDING:
  Problem: Free FII/DII tools either show raw tables (ScanX, 5paisa) or require registration (TradersCockpit) or payment (Trendlyne).
  Users: Indian retail swing traders and investors.
  Model: Free open-source (AGPL). No paid tier planned.

MOAT OPPORTUNITIES:

  1. Telegram alerts (Low moat — trivial to copy. But first-mover advantage among free tools.)
  2. AI summary (Medium moat — needs LLM integration. Competitors won't add it while data stays free.)
  3. Toolchain integration (Medium-High moat — tying into user's personal portfolio + sentiment data.
      Competitors don't have access to user's holdings across multiple brokers.)
  4. Open-source AGPL (Low moat — prevents monetization by competitors but doesn't retain users.)
